Why age is the right lever: contactability decays fast. The FCC estimates ~35M U.S. numbers are disconnected and reassigned each year (~10% of all numbers), and ~42.9% of people change phone number within 12 months — so by 1–2 years a record is materially less likely to reach the intended person, and by 2+ years many numbers are dead. That decay, not any status flag, is the case for refreshing the oldest records first. Sources: FCC, Reassigned Numbers Database; IndustrySelect, “The High Cost of Bad Contact Data.”

T1, T2 and Unknown carry virtually all the stale data. Of the 1,148,450 records that are 2+ years old, 99% sit in T1, T2, or Unknown. The 2026 tiers (T3, T6, T8 — 289,296 records combined) are ~100% under one year, confirming they came from recent pulls and are the higher-intent cohort for active dialing.

Tagging hygiene is a real gap: 448,492 records (14%) have no identifiable source tier. One in seven records can't be attributed to a sourcing batch, which limits how precisely we can target refresh spend. Worth tightening SOURCE/TAGS capture at intake.

Read this as a data-quality signal, not an age signal. Because a number’s NUMBER CREATED AT updates each time it’s refreshed or returned through the domain, the recorded date reflects the latest pull rather than the original skiptrace — so age isn’t the right lens for wrong numbers. What the rate does show is where call lists degrade fastest — Leave the Key (20%), Pillar (11%) and Cava (10%) lead — warranting a source-quality review at intake. The case for refreshing aged records rests on industry contactability decay (prior page), a lever separate from this rate.

Blind spot: Patriot Home Buyers and Rapid Fire HB (1,267,014 properties, 41% of the book) log no wrong-number STATUS. We can't quantify their caller-productivity drag until STATUS capture is enabled in the dialer.

Recommendation & Next Steps
Run a prioritized re-skiptrace of the oldest T1/T2 cohorts, starting with the six books that hold the most 2+ year data, and close the tagging and STATUS-capture gaps in parallel.

Why this, in order

  1. Refresh oldest-first. 1,148,450 records are 2+ years old and overwhelmingly T1/T2 — the largest, stalest, highest-recovery cohort.
  2. Review source quality where wrong-number rates run high. Leave the Key, Pillar and Cava carry the highest wrong-number rates — a source-quality review at intake will do more there than a re-skiptrace alone.
  3. Turn on STATUS capture for Patriot and Rapid Fire so the 41% of the book we're blind to becomes measurable.
  4. Fix tagging at intake to shrink the 14% Unknown-source cohort and sharpen future targeting.
